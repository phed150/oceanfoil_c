Oceanfoil is a UK-based design company focused on the development of aerofoil designs suitable for installation on sea and ocean-going merchant vessels.

Oceanfoil has partnered with two of Europe’s leading research-based universities to redevelop and improve designs for wind-assisted propulsion to augment conventional, mechanical power for ocean transport ships.

Oceanfoil aims to bring together the necessary skills, expertise and funding to develop and exploit the commercial potential of designs for aerofoil- assisted propulsion systems for the ocean-going transport sector.

Oceanfoil has partnered with universities, testing centres and designers to bring designs for an aerofoil system to a production-ready standard,adapting the designs with the benefit of modern computer modelling and composite materials.

T Alun Evans CMG
Chairman

Alun Evans served in what became the Foreign and Commonwealth office, serving abroad in Rangoon, Singapore, Geneva and Pretoria. Latterly, he served as Director responsible for IT and Communications in London. After leaving the FCO he advised international airlines such as British Airways on international affairs. From 2001 to 2012 he was a non-executive director of JP Morgan (Fleming) Asian Investment Trust and has been Vice Chairman of the British Iranian Chamber of Commerce for over a decade.

Alun Evans was educated at University College, Oxford and held a commission in the King’s Own Yorkshire Light Infantry. He was appointed a Companion of the Order of St. Michael and St. George in 2004.

John Richardson
Executive Director, Engineering and Production

John Richardson is a consultant engineer who has overseen plant operations, site maintenance and energy management for a wide variety of facilities in the chemical and gas industries. Throughout his career John has implemented initiatives and deployed systems which have optimised either plant function or site maintenance.

John Richardson is a Chartered Engineer and the managing director of a carbon management consultancy. John served for 22 years as an Engineer Officer in the Royal Navy Reserve, spending time at sea as an Engineer and Seaman Officer. John has been active in producing engineering solutions to off-shore applications

Captain Roger Francis
Marine Consultant

Captain Francis is a retired deep sea pilot, having trained at Trinity House, prior to which he captained numerous merchant vessels for Stephenson Clarke Shipping and served as a Chief Officer on ships of the Geest Line and Harrisons. In 1987, Captain Francis was selected as the Master of the merchant vessel M.V. Ashington during sea trials of the ‘Walker Wingsail’ aerofoil design, completing several voyages with the system in place in the course of its evaluation. Oceanfoil now controls the rights to the aerofoil designed by John Walker and this forms the basis for Oceanfoil’s improved designs.

Roger Francis possesses a Master’s Foreign Going certificate and Deep Sea Pilotage License, issued by Trinity House. He is a member of the Nautical Institute and a Governor of the R.N.L.I.

Robert Vincent
Chief Designer

Robert Vincent is a technical designer, specialising in the computer aided design and drafting of marine technologies and naval architecture. Robert Vincent has extensive experience in the design and modelling of aerofoil technology for the marine sector as well as smaller vessels powered solely by the technology. He has been closely involved in the progressive refinement of aerofoil designs throughout a career spanning thirty years.

Making use of computational modelling techniques, and adapting the designs to modern, composite materials and production processes, Robert is instrumental to fully realising the value of this technology to the transport fleet.
