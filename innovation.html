The challenge of limiting the cost and environmental impact of medium and long-distance transport is changing the way companies think about the design and operation of their ships.

Oceanfoil brings together expertise in design, testing, software development and maritime engineering to modernise ideas and designs developed at a time when the cost of fuel made them uneconomical to produce..

An aerofoil makes use of the same forces that give a wing lift, but converts this to thrust for a vessel. Oceanfoilâ€™s refined aerofoil design currently delivers up to 15% reduction in fuel consumption in suitable conditions. With the aid of modelling and testing using computational fluid dynamics, Oceanfoil is developing the design further, increasing the performance of the aerofoil and updating its controls for the modern fleet.

Oceanfoil is refining and redeveloping designs that were first tested aboard a merchant vessel in the 1980s, the tests showing that the aerofoil delivered better engine efficiency and reduced the amount of fuel used by the ship over the course of several voyages by a margin of up to 15% in suitable wind conditions. When the cost of bunker oil fell to under $50 per tonne in the late 1980s, and remained below $100 per tonne until the end of the century, the savings offered were not sufficient to be pursued commercially and the design was not put into production.
